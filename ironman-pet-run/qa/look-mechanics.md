# Look mechanics — IronMan

The compact armored mascot keeps both boots and its circular cyan chest reactor anchored on a stable baseline. Its helmeted head and glowing eye slits lead attention; the neck and upper torso follow by a small, rigid turn without warping the helmet or armor plates. The eye slits reshape and shift naturally within the black faceplate, while the cyan reactor remains centered and visible.

- **000 up:** eye slits and helmet brow angle upward; chin lifts slightly and upper torso follows back a little.
- **090 screen-right:** helmet and eye slits turn unmistakably toward the viewer's right; the right helmet rim and shoulder become more visible, with the left side slightly occluded.
- **180 down:** eye slits angle downward; helmet dips and shoulders compress subtly forward.
- **270 screen-left:** helmet and eye slits turn unmistakably toward the viewer's left; the left helmet rim and shoulder become more visible, with the right side slightly occluded.

Each 22.5-degree step uses an even, restrained change in helmet turn, eye-slit aim, and shoulder follow-through. There is no whole-sprite rotation, tilt, skew, scale change, or prop. The clock loop progresses smoothly from row 9 (up through down-right) into row 10 (down through up-left) and back to up.
